Geography Zones of Chad

Chad spans the Sahara, Sahel, and Savannah in a single nation. Five ecological zones, each demanding different preparation, different respect, and different understanding. Start with the land.

ChadTrip is organized around geography because geography explains everything. Where people live, how they move, what routes are possible, what preparation is required — it all starts with understanding the land. Click any zone below to begin your orientation.

Northern Chad

The Sahara

Desert scale. Extreme remoteness. Expedition logistics. Silence and horizon stretching beyond imagination. The ultimate expedition terrain.
Central Chad

The Sahel

Transition landscapes. Semi-arid routes. Cultural corridors connecting the desert to the green south. The living middle ground.
Southern Chad

The Savannah

Greener landscapes. Agricultural zones. Cultural density and wildlife corridors. N'Djamena and the accessible south.
Western Border

Lake Chad

History and water. Environmental change on a generational scale. Local communities shaped by Africa's most dynamic lake.
Mythic-Tier

Tibesti Mountains

Volcanic mountains. Expedition-only travel. Emi Koussi at 3,415m. One of Africa's most dramatic and least-visited landscapes.

Chad's story is land and scale. Five ecological zones in one country. Understand the geography, and the rest — the routes, the people, the preparation — all follows.
